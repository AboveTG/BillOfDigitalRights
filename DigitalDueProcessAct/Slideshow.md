📊 Minnesota Digital Due Process Act

Legislative Presentation Deck


Slide 1 — Title

Minnesota Digital Due Process Act

Protecting Constitutional Due Process in the Age of Automated Government Decision-Making

Proposed state-level legislative framework
Focus: Human accountability in automated decisions
Technology-neutral governance standard


Slide 2 — The Core Problem

Government decisions are increasingly influenced by:

AI systems
Predictive analytics
Risk scoring tools
Automated eligibility systems
Algorithmic classification systems
Key Issue:

Individuals may experience consequences based on predictions instead of evidence


Slide 3 — What Has Changed

Traditional model:

Human decision-maker
Evidence-based evaluation
Clear accountability

Modern model:

Machine generates recommendation
Human may defer to system output
Decision logic becomes opaque
Risk:

Automation becomes de facto decision authority


Slide 4 — Why This Matters

Without safeguards:

Decisions may rely on statistical inference alone
Individuals may not know why they were affected
Errors become difficult to detect or challenge
Accountability becomes diffuse
Core concern:

Due process must remain meaningful in digital systems


Slide 5 — What the Bill Does NOT Do

This proposal:

Does NOT ban AI
Does NOT restrict law enforcement tools
Does NOT require source code disclosure
Does NOT stop predictive analytics
Does NOT create new data ownership rights
It is not anti-technology

It is pro-accountability


Slide 6 — What the Bill DOES Do

Establishes four core safeguards:

Human review required for consequential decisions
Independent supporting evidence required
Notice when automated systems are used
Right to review/appeal


Slide 7 — Core Principle

“No individual should be subject to a material adverse government action solely on the basis of an automated prediction.”

Translation:
AI can assist
AI cannot be the final authority


Slide 8 — Constitutional Alignment

Built on existing principles:

Due Process (14th Amendment)
Fair procedure requirements
Equal protection principles
Accountability in government action
Key point:

This is an extension of existing constitutional doctrine, not a new rights framework


Slide 9 — Real-World Applications

Applies to:

Benefits eligibility systems
Fraud detection systems
Licensing decisions
Enforcement prioritization tools
Risk scoring systems
Does NOT block:
Investigative leads
Intelligence analysis
Administrative support tools


Slide 10 — Why Now

Because systems are rapidly scaling:

Faster decisions
Larger datasets
More predictive models
Increasing automation in government workflows
Risk trajectory:

Automation shifts from “support tool” → “decision authority”


Slide 11 — Policy Balance

This bill is designed to balance:

Innovation

Preserves AI use in government

Efficiency

Maintains automated processing

Rights

Ensures human accountability in final decisions


Slide 12 — Closing Message

Technology may assist government decision-making, but constitutional responsibility must remain human.


Legislative Objective:

Ensure that modernization of government does not reduce the practical meaning of due process.
