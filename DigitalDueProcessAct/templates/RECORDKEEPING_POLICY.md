# Recordkeeping & Disclosure Guidance — {SHORT TITLE}

Purpose: provide minimal, practical recordkeeping and public-summary guidance that balances oversight with legitimate confidentiality needs.

Minimum retention: retain records documenting Human Review, supporting evidence, and system identifiers for five years unless otherwise required by law.

Public summary: agencies should publish an annual (or biennial) summary describing systems in use, purposes, approximate volumes, and the agency’s Human Review process. Redact sensitive or protected details (trade secrets, ongoing investigations) as allowed by law.

Disclosure: do not require disclosure of privileged or law-enforcement-sensitive material; where disclosure is permitted, provide a non-technical summary sufficient to enable meaningful review.
